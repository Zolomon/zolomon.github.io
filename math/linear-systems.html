Linear Systems

$$\text{1.1) Specify the angular frequency, frequency and period for the sinus waves:}$$ $$a)\ u(t) = 3 \sin (2t - 5),$$ $$b)\ u(t) = 50 \sin (100\pi t + 1).$$

To answer a) & b), we just need to remember the constituent parts of the formula for an undampened oscillator, given by: $u(t) = A sin(\omega t + \alpha)$.

Here, $A$ is the $\textbf{amplitude}$, $\omega$ is the $\textbf{angular frequency}$, and $\alpha$ is the $\textbf{phase}$. The frequency and period are defined as: $$ \textbf{Period: } T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}, $$ $$ \textbf{Frequency: } f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}.$$ Thus, by looking and substituting the parts from u(t) with the definitions for the frequency and period, we receive the following:

For a): $$ \textbf{Angular frequency} = \omega = 2, $$ $$ \textbf{Period} = T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = \frac{2\pi}{2} = \pi, $$ $$ \textbf{Frequency} = f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1}{\pi}. $$

For b) $$ \textbf{Angular frequency} = \omega = 100\pi, $$ $$ \textbf{Period} = T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = \frac{2\pi}{100\pi} = \frac{1}{50}, $$ $$ \textbf{Frequency} = f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{50}} = \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{50}{1} = 50. $$
